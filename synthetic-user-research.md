## Cross-Segment Synthesis

### Top 10 Cross-Segment Insights

1. **The 30-Second Rule** — Every segment expects to see a creation within 30-60 seconds of opening the app. Any friction before the first "wow" moment is fatal, especially for beginners, teens, and kids.

2. **Text on Images is a Universal Need** — From Linda's birthday invitations to Jake's YouTube thumbnails, nearly every persona needs to add text to AI-generated images. Pure image generation without text/design tools is a half-product. The Canvas feature is not a nice-to-have — it's essential.

3. **Consistency is the #1 Professional Barrier** — Segments 3-5 all cite style/character/brand consistency as a top pain point. Without it, Craftit remains a toy for occasional use rather than a workflow tool.

4. **Reference Image is the Killer Feature** — Across all segments, the ability to use their own images (products, food, sketches, photos) as starting points is what separates Craftit from "another AI art generator." This bridges "cool AI images" and "useful for MY life."

5. **"Is This My Work?" Identity Question** — From Zara (ethics) to Jake (brand) to Linda (pride), users across all segments want to feel ownership over creations. The tool must feel like it amplifies THEM, not replaces them.

6. **Price Sensitivity Spans 10x** — Willingness to pay ranges from $0 (Aisha/Tommy) to $100/month (Nina's team). A single pricing tier won't work. Need: free → casual ($5-10) → pro ($20-30) → team ($50+).

7. **Templates/Workflows > Blank Canvas** — Beginners and business owners don't want creative freedom — they want guided workflows. "What do you want to make?" → category → template → customize. Only professionals want the blank canvas.

8. **Video is the Differentiator, Images are the Entry** — Users discover Craftit for images but stay for video. Short video generation is what competitors (Canva, basic AI tools) can't easily match. Auto-mode long video is the moonshot feature.

9. **Community is Double-Edged** — Aisha and Tommy love it (social validation). Priya fears it (exposing her "secret tool"). Professionals ignore it. Community should be opt-in and never feel like a requirement.

10. **Speed = Respect for User's Time** — Every persona, without exception, mentions speed. For a teen, it's impatience. For a business owner, it's "I don't have time." For a creator, it's "I need volume." Fast generation isn't a technical flex — it's a core value proposition.

### Critical UX Moments (Make-or-Break)

1. **First Generation Result (ALL segments)** — If the first image/video isn't impressive, users leave immediately. This moment carries the entire product. Optimize default quality, pre-fill good prompts, show best-case examples.

2. **"How Do I Edit This?" (Beginners, SMB)** — The moment after generation when a user wants to change ONE thing. If there's no edit path, they feel stuck. Canvas feature directly addresses this.

3. **"It Doesn't Look Like MY Brand/Product" (Creators, SMB, Professional)** — The moment a professional user realizes AI output is generic, not theirs. Reference images + style saving + brand kits are the answer.

4. **Sign-Up Wall Timing** — Requiring sign-up BEFORE first generation is a conversion killer for Beginners and Kids. Let them generate one image free, then gate.

5. **Credit Exhaustion Moment** — When free credits run out. This is where pricing, value communication, and upgrade UX either convert or lose the user forever.

6. **Share Flow** — The moment a user wants to share their creation. Every extra tap between "done" and "shared on Instagram/WhatsApp" is friction. One-tap share is essential.
