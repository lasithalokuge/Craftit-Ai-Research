How to Create AI UGC Videos — Complete Step-by-Step Guide
A comprehensive, practical guide synthesized from all 20 videos we analyzed. Follow these steps to create high-converting AI UGC ads.

STEP 1 Find a Proven Ad to Model

Never start from scratch. Every top creator begins by finding ads that are already working. You're not copying — you're learning what psychology and structure converts, then applying it to your product.

🔍 Where to Find Winners

Facebook Ad Library — Search competitor brands. Look for ads running 30+ days (= profitable). If the same video appears multiple times, it's a proven winner.

TikTok Top Ads — Go to ads.tiktok.com → filter by region, product category, time period (180 days). Sort by views and CTR.

TikTok Creative Center — Browse trending ad formats and top-performing creatives by industry.

🎯 What to Look For

• Ads that have been running for 30+ days (signals profitability)

• Same video appearing multiple times in the library

• High view count + high engagement rate

• Brands with 500+ active ads = they're scaling hard

• UGC-style (selfie/casual) over polished brand content

💾 How to Save

• Download the video — use "Ad Library" Chrome extension or right-click on TikTok

• Screenshot key frames — pause at powerful moments, save to a folder

• Build a swipe file — save every good ad in Notion or Google Drive for future reference

STEP 2 Extract & Write the Script

The script is the single most important element. A great script with mediocre visuals outperforms beautiful visuals with a weak script every time. Here's how to write one that converts:

🔤 Extract the Winning Script

Option A: Upload the competitor's video to Google Gemini → ask it to transcribe word-for-word. Gemini is the only tool that reliably extracts captions from uploaded video.

Option B: Use Loom to screen-record the ad (auto-transcribes), then copy the transcript.

Option C: Use Script Talk Audit to download transcripts from TikTok videos directly.

✍️ Adapt the Script for Your Product

Paste the transcript into Claude (preferred for scripts) or ChatGPT with this kind of prompt:

"Here's a transcript from a winning UGC ad. Adapt this script for my product [name]. Keep the same emotional structure and psychology, but change all product-specific details. My product is [description]. Target audience: [who]. Key benefits: [list]. Tone: ultra-casual, like texting a friend. Length: 25-30 seconds for 3 different speakers."

Pro tip: Generate 5+ script variations with different angles — anti-aging, convenience, science/tech, emotional, before/after. Success often comes on the 7th or 8th script.

📐 The Script Structure That Converts

Every winning UGC ad follows this 5-part structure:

HOOK1-3s"Remembering to take all my supplements is a lot sometimes" — Relatable, stops the scroll
PROBLEM3-5s"I was spending $200/month on 6 different bottles and half the time forgetting them" — Agitate the pain
SOLUTION10-15s"Then I found [product] — it's everything in one scoop" — Show product naturally in use
PROOF5-10s"After 3 weeks my energy is up and I haven't missed a day" — Personal result or social proof
CTA3-5s"You've got to try this for yourself — link's in my bio" — Clear, specific action

Split the script across 3 speakers — each reads 1-2 parts. Multi-avatar montages outperform single-person ads.

STEP 3 Generate AI Actors & Product Images

Now create the visual elements — AI people who will deliver your script, and product shots for B-roll.

🧑 Create AI Actors (3 Methods)

Method 1: Use a Platform Library (Fastest)

Go to Arcads or Spiel → browse actor library → filter by age, gender, accessories. Choose actors who look like they'd naturally use your product (gym wear for supplements, etc.).

Method 2: Generate from Scratch (Most Control)

Use Nano Banana Pro (via ElevenLabs or Freepik) with prompts like:

"25-year-old woman in casual athleisure, holding phone, front camera selfie angle, natural lighting, 9:16 portrait, photorealistic"

Critical: Include "phone, front camera, selfie" in your prompt — this creates the authentic UGC feel.

Method 3: Use Yourself + Character Swap

Record yourself holding the product → use Kling 3.0 motion control to swap your face with an AI character. Most realistic result.

📸 Create Product Shots

Product in Hands

Upload your actor image + clean product photo → prompt: "Place [product] in the person's right hand. Match lighting to environment." Use the product grid trick: first generate a 3×3 grid of product angles, then use as reference so AI can adjust naturally.

Product B-Roll

Generate lifestyle context shots — product on a desk, in a bathroom, being unboxed. Use specific prompts:

"Macro close-up of [product] bottle on marble bathroom counter, morning sunlight, soft bokeh background, photorealistic, warm tones"

Consistency Rule

⚠️ Always edit from the base actor image, never from an already-edited version. Chaining edits compounds errors and breaks character consistency.

STEP 4 Generate Video Clips (A-Roll + B-Roll)

This is where still images become video. You need two types of footage — and treating them differently is what separates amateur from professional AI ads.

🗣️ A-Roll (Talking Head Clips)

The actor speaking directly to camera, delivering the script.

Best tools:

  • Arcads — Select actor, paste script segment, choose TTS or speech-to-speech. ~1 min per clip.
  • Spiel Custom Mode — Upload first frame + script line, select voice, add motion prompt ("talking naturally with relaxed hand gestures"). Up to 60s per clip.
  • Google Veo 3.1 — Upload start frame, include script in prompt. 8-10s clips. Free unlimited on top tier.

Speech-to-Speech (game changer): Record yourself reading the script with your desired tone and inflection → Arcads clones your tonality onto the AI actor. Dramatically more realistic than text-to-speech.

Voice settings: Speed 0.9-1.2x · Stability ~0.4 (for natural variation) · Style exaggeration ~0.1-0.5

🎬 B-Roll (Supporting Clips)

Product demos, lifestyle shots, close-ups — shown while the voiceover continues.

Best tools:

  • Kling 2.5-3.0 — Image-to-video, 5-8s clips. Write hyper-specific prompts describing camera movements and lighting. Most realistic.
  • Spiel Easy Mode — Upload first frame + motion prompt. Better for complex motion. Up to 8s.
  • CapCut Commerce Pro — Paste Shopify URL → auto-generates 10-30 product video clips. Free.

Prompting tip: Be hyper-specific — describe exact hand movements, camera angles, lighting, ambient sound:

"She slowly opens the box, lifts product out with both hands. Camera pushes in from medium to close-up. Warm kitchen lighting, shallow depth of field."

Start+End Frame technique: Create both frames in Nano Banana, upload to Kling → AI generates the motion between. Maximum control.

STEP 5 Audio — Voice, Music & Sound Effects

Audio is often overlooked but adds 15-20% engagement. Here's how to layer it properly.

🎙️ Voice Consistency

If you generated A-roll across multiple clips (different tools or sessions), the voices may sound different.

Fix: Export all audio → run through ElevenLabs Voice Changer → select one consistent target voice → re-sync in editor.

This unifies all clips to sound like the same person.

🎵 Background Music

Use Suno to generate royalty-free music matched to your ad's mood — upbeat for energy, calm for testimonials.

Volume: Keep music quiet — it should support, not compete with the voiceover.

TikTok tip: Remove music before export → add trending TikTok sounds in-app instead. This boosts algorithmic reach.

🔊 Sound Effects

Use ElevenLabs Sound Effects (free) → type a description ("unboxing sound, cardboard opening") → select length → generate.

Add subtle SFX at key moments: product reveal, before/after transition, CTA emphasis.

Google Veo 3.1 also includes built-in sound effects in generated video.

STEP 6 Edit & Assemble the Final Video

Bring everything together. CapCut is the universal choice across creators — free, intuitive, and has great caption features.

🎞️ Assembly Order

  1. Set format to 9:16 (vertical) — this is mandatory for TikTok, Reels, Shorts
  2. Layer A-roll clips chronologically — Speaker 1 (Hook + Problem) → Speaker 2 (Solution) → Speaker 3 (Proof + CTA)
  3. Overlay B-roll on top of A-roll — when the speaker mentions the product, cut to product B-roll while voiceover continues
  4. Add auto-captions — CapCut has styled caption templates. Pick one that's bold and easy to read. Most short-form is watched without sound.
  5. Add background music — keep it very low, just enough to fill silence
  6. Add CTA overlay at the end — "Shop Now" text + arrow pointing to the link position (bottom of screen on TikTok)
  7. Speed up B-roll by ~20% — this hides AI imperfections and creates a more dynamic feel
  8. Cover lip-sync issues — if any A-roll has lip sync problems, cut to B-roll at that moment

✅ Editing Best Practices

• Keep pacing HIGH — lots of small clips, frequent cuts (every 2-4 seconds)

• Never show just a talking head for more than 5 seconds — cut to B-roll

• Use speed ramping on product reveals (slow → fast)

• Add subtle zoom/pan to static product shots

• Keep total length 20-30s for paid ads, up to 60s for organic

❌ Common Mistakes to Avoid

• Music too loud — overpowers voiceover

• Too much text on screen — keep captions only

• Slow pacing — if it feels slow, it's too slow

• No B-roll — just talking head = boring, scroll-past

• Polished brand feel — should feel like UGC, not a commercial

• Forgetting captions — 80%+ watch without sound

STEP 7 Export, Publish & Test Variations

One video isn't enough. The real money comes from systematic testing of variations.

📤 Export Specs

TikTok / Reels / Shorts: 9:16, keep text in safe zone (avoid top/bottom 15%)

Facebook / Instagram Feed: 4:5 aspect ratio

YouTube / LinkedIn: 16:9 standard

Audio: Normalize to -14 LUFS

🔄 Create Variations

Method 1: Script × Actor matrix

Take your winning script → generate with 10+ different actors. Take your winning actor → give them 10+ different scripts. Test all combinations.

Method 2: Hook swapping

Keep the body + CTA the same, but test 5-10 different hooks. The hook is the single biggest lever.

📊 Testing Framework

Launch: Manual Sales Campaign (CBO) on Meta, or post organically on TikTok

Track: Hook rate (3s views), VTR (completion), CTR, ROAS

Scale winners: When you find a script+actor that works, create 10+ more variations of that combo

TikTok: Post the same video 20 times — algorithm shows different audiences

The testing math: 5 scripts × 5 actors × 5 hooks = 125 unique variations. This is how top advertisers consistently find winners. Speed of iteration > quality of any single creative.

Implementation Roadmap — EDUCATE
Four phases to build Craftit's educational video engine.

WEEKS 1-4 Phase 1: Foundation

  • URL-to-review pipeline — paste product URL → auto-generate review video with features/pros/cons/verdict
  • Topic-to-explainer pipeline — type topic + key points → Hook→Explain→CTA video
  • 4 pedagogical frameworks — auto-select based on video type
  • Visual matching engine — auto-find/generate images per script line
  • MVP output: Simple explainer and review videos from URL or topic

WEEKS 5-8 Phase 2: Document & Avatar

  • Document-to-video — PDF/PPT upload → structured training video with chapters
  • AI avatar presenter — consistent avatar per brand, lip-synced
  • Brand kit auto-detection — paste company URL → extract logo, colors, fonts
  • Screen recording integration — record workflow → auto-annotated tutorial
  • Output: Corporate training videos and branded tutorials

WEEKS 9-12 Phase 3: Scale & Intelligence

  • Multi-scenario generation — 5-10 script angles per topic
  • Chat-based editing — "change to Spanish", "remove subtitles", "make it shorter"
  • One-click translation — 175+ languages with lip-sync
  • Interactive elements — quizzes, click-forward pauses, knowledge checks
  • Output: Multi-language educational content at scale

WEEKS 13-16 Phase 4: Learning Platform

  • LMS integration — embed in learning management systems
  • Analytics dashboard — completion rates, drop-off, engagement heatmaps
  • SEO optimization — auto-generated metadata, chapters, descriptions
  • Content series — auto-plan multi-video courses from one document
  • Output: Full educational content platform integration

How to Create AI Educational Videos — Step-by-Step Guide
A practical guide synthesized from all 20 videos. Covers the three main approaches.

APPROACH 1 URL-to-Video (Product Reviews, 5 min)

  • Step 1: Copy any product URL (Amazon, Shopify, etc.)
  • Step 2: Paste into Craftit → AI scrapes product name, images, features, price
  • Step 3: Select video type (Review, Feature Walkthrough, Demo)
  • Step 4: Choose avatar + voice (or go faceless)
  • Step 5: AI generates 5+ script scenarios (different angles)
  • Step 6: Pick favorites → render → export per platform

Tools today: TopView AI ($49/mo, 600 videos) · Fliki (free tier) · Creatify

APPROACH 2 Topic-to-Video (Explainers, 10 min)

  • Step 1: Type your topic + 2-3 key points to cover
  • Step 2: Specify audience ("beginners", "gym-goers", "developers")
  • Step 3: AI generates structured script using Hook→Problem→Explain→Proof→CTA
  • Step 4: AI auto-matches visuals (diagrams, images, animations) to each script line
  • Step 5: Review the plan — adjust for clarity, not technical editing
  • Step 6: Generate → pick from 5+ variations → render

Tools today: InVideo AI ($100/mo) · HeyGen ($24/mo) · CreateStudio

APPROACH 3 Document-to-Video (Training, 15 min)

  • Step 1: Upload PDF, PPT, or training manual
  • Step 2: Set training goal + audience level
  • Step 3: AI parses document → creates chapter structure
  • Step 4: AI generates avatar presenter + slides from document content
  • Step 5: Apply brand kit (auto from company URL or manual)
  • Step 6: Review chapters → generate → translate to additional languages

Tools today: HeyGen (PDF/PPT to video) · Synthesia ($22/mo)
