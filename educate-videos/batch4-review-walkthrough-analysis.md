## Video 2: "Create Amazon Product Review Video with AI | URL to Video" (Fliki)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-UuvqhmzNA
**Tool Featured:** Fliki AI (+ VidIQ for keyword research)
**Style:** Detailed tutorial with strategic framing (keyword research → creation → editing)

### Complete Workflow
1. **Keyword Research (VidIQ)**
   - Find niche keyword with good search volume + medium competition
   - Validate on YouTube: check if small channels rank for the term
   - Example: "best gaming laptop under 50,000" — found 8.9K channel ranking
2. **Product Selection (Amazon)**
   - Search Amazon with same keyword
   - Filter by reviews (only promote good products)
   - Copy product URL
3. **Video Creation (Fliki AI)**
   - Click "New File" → select "Video"
   - Enter name, choose language, accent
   - Choose aspect ratio (landscape for YouTube, portrait for Shorts/IG)
   - Under "Start with" → select "Product"
   - Paste Amazon product URL
   - Select duration (e.g., 3 minutes max)
   - Choose template (product review template available)
   - Click Submit → AI generates first-cut video
4. **Editing (Fliki Editor)**
   - Scene-by-scene editing on left sidebar
   - AI auto-pulls media from Amazon listing
   - Adjust background music volume (lower so voiceover is clear)
   - Add/remove subtitles
   - Add opening scene with custom text + stock video
   - Add AI avatar layer (close-up or transparent background options)
   - Generate lip-synced avatar video
   - Change voiceover artist or clone your own voice
   - Apply voice to all scenes at once
   - Change avatar gender/appearance from gallery
   - Add closing CTA scene with price + "click the link below"
   - Customize fonts, sizes, positioning
5. **Export** at highest resolution

### Tools/AI Models
- **VidIQ**: YouTube keyword research, competition analysis, search volume
- **Fliki AI**: Primary video creation tool
  - URL-to-video (product page → video)
  - Also supports: prompt/idea, script, blog post, PPT, tweet → video
  - AI voiceover with multiple artists/accents/languages
  - Voice cloning
  - AI avatars with lip-sync generation
  - Scene-based editor
  - Stock media library
  - Template system

### Required Inputs
- Target keyword (for SEO strategy)
- Amazon product URL
- Language + accent preferences
- Aspect ratio
- Duration preference
- Template choice
- Editorial decisions (opening, CTA, avatar choice)

### Video Structure Pattern (Generated + Edited)
1. **Custom opening scene**: "I'll show you the best gaming laptop under 50,000"
2. **Product introduction**: Name, key specs
3. **Feature breakdown**: Scene per feature, Amazon images as visuals
4. **Spec highlights**: Display, processor, special features
5. **CTA closing**: Price display, "click the link below for best discount"

### Key Insights
- **Most strategic approach**: Combines SEO research → product selection → video creation
- The keyword-first workflow ensures the video targets actual demand
- Fliki's multi-input support (URL, script, blog, PPT) is versatile
- Voice cloning creates brand consistency
- Scene-by-scene granular editing gives full control
- Avatar adds presenter feel; lip-sync makes it more engaging
- Template system reduces setup time significantly

4. **Specs/Technical Details** — Display, processor, dimensions, etc.

### 6. What Craftit Can Learn

#### Immediate Opportunities
1. **URL-to-video for EDUCATE category**: Product URL → AI-generated review video is the #1 pattern. Craftit should support pasting a product/service URL and auto-generating a review or walkthrough video.

2. **Workflow templates matter**: InVideo's "Workflows" feature (pre-built structures for explainers, ads, reviews) is its killer feature. Craftit should offer EDUCATE-specific templates: Product Review, How-To Guide, FAQ Explainer, Industry Overview.

3. **Multiple script variants**: TopView's 3-6 scenario generation per product is compelling for users who want to test different angles. Craftit could offer "generate 3 versions" for any EDUCATE video.

4. **Chat-based editing**: InVideo's "type to edit" approach (remove subtitles, change language, swap clips) removes the need for timeline editing. This is the future of video editing UX.

5. **SEO-integrated workflow**: Fliki video (V2) showed the most strategic approach — keyword research THEN video creation. Craftit could integrate keyword/topic validation to help users create content that will actually rank.

#### Structural Insights
6. **Scene-based architecture**: All tools use a scene/slide model internally. Each scene = 1 feature/point + matching visual + voiceover segment. This modular structure enables easy editing and rearrangement.

7. **Avatar as optional layer**: Avatars add perceived credibility to reviews. Should be an optional enhancement, not required.

8. **Voice cloning for brand consistency**: Fliki's voice cloning means the same "reviewer" voice across all content. Important for channel branding.

9. **Multi-platform aspect ratios**: Every tool offers 9:16 vs 16:9 toggle. EDUCATE videos need both YouTube (16:9) and Shorts/Reels/TikTok (9:16) versions.

10. **E-commerce platform breadth**: Supporting Amazon, Shopify, eBay, AliExpress, TikTok Shop, and a Chrome extension fallback for unsupported platforms covers the full affiliate/review ecosystem.

#### Competitive Positioning
- **TopView AI** ($49/mo) and **Fliki** own the URL-to-review-video niche
- **InVideo AI** ($100/mo) owns the general-purpose prompt-to-video space
- **Gap**: No tool specifically optimizes for EDUCATE content (reviews + explainers + FAQs + walkthroughs) as a unified category
- **Craftit opportunity**: Purpose-built EDUCATE workflows that combine URL parsing, knowledge structuring, and pedagogical video frameworks (not just "paste link, get hype video")

#### Quality vs. Automation Spectrum
- Fully automated (TopView): Fast, cheap, but generic/hype-oriented scripts
- Semi-automated (Fliki): Good balance of speed + editorial control
- Orchestrated (InVideo): Most flexible, highest quality, but more configuration
- Manual assembly (Grok/ClipChamp): Maximum control, most effort, creative content only

**Craftit should target the Fliki/InVideo sweet spot**: automated enough to be fast, but with enough structure and customization to produce genuinely informative EDUCATE content rather than just promotional hype.
