## Video 1: "Create AI Explainer Videos Like a PRO – EASY TUTORIAL"

- **Creator:** Ian Pella (motion graphic designer)
- **Tool:** CreateStudio (desktop software)
- **Style:** Character-based animated explainer with stock images

### Workflow
1. **Open AI Explainer Video Builder** inside CreateStudio
2. **Write a detailed prompt** specifying: duration (30 sec), company type (general contractor), target audience (homeowners/busy families), tone (friendly, professional, solution-oriented), script structure (hook → problem → solution → CTA), business name
3. **Pick a character** from library (chose contractor-looking character)
4. **Select music style** ("stomp music")
5. **Choose voiceover** settings
6. **Generate project** — AI builds full timeline with scenes, text, voiceover, character animations
7. **Review & refine** — swap stock backgrounds for real/relevant photos, customize character colors to brand palette (overalls, shirt matching brand colors), adjust text boxes and rectangles to brand colors
8. **Polish** — add drop shadows, crop effects, reposition elements, ensure text timing matches voiceover

### Inputs Required
- Company/product description
- Target audience
- Desired tone
- Script structure framework
- Business name
- Brand colors
- Duration target

### Key Insights
- **"Hook → Problem → Solution → CTA" is the blueprint** — creator explicitly calls this THE formula for explainer videos
- AI generates a usable first draft ("blueprint") but manual refinement is essential
- Brand consistency (colors, character customization) elevates quality significantly
- Real photos mixed with animated characters creates professional look
- Text timing with voiceover is critical detail

## Video 2: "This FREE AI Tool Makes Explainer Videos in 1 Click!" (NotebookLM)

- **Creator:** Tech reviewer
- **Tool:** Google NotebookLM — Video Overviews feature
- **Style:** AI-generated narrated slideshow with dynamic visuals, diagrams, quotes

### Workflow
1. **Go to notebooklm.google.com** and create a new notebook
2. **Add sources** — YouTube videos, website links, PDFs, Google Drive docs, pasted text, markdown, audio
3. **Click "Video Overview"** under Studio panel (one click)
4. **Wait 5-10 minutes** for generation
5. **Preview or download** the generated video (MP4)
6. **Share** with students, embed in content, etc.

### Inputs Required
- Source materials only (YouTube links, docs, PDFs, websites)
- No script writing, no manual editing, no voiceover work

### Key Insights
- **Zero-effort explainer creation** — literally one click after adding sources
- Creates dynamic slides (not just bullet points) with diagrams, pulled images, quotes, and numbers from source docs
- Output is ~7-8 minutes from 2-3 sources
- Drawback: takes 5-10 minutes to generate, limited customization
- Positioned as learning/studying tool but repurposable for content
- Available in 80+ languages as of August 2025
- Quality depends entirely on quality of input sources

## Video 3: "How To Make Explainer Videos That Go Viral in 2026"

- **Creator:** YouTube growth educator
- **Style:** White background + images/clips + text overlay + voiceover ("every X explained" format)
- **Tools:** ChatGPT, ElevenLabs or Google AI Studio, Premiere Pro (or CapCut)

### Workflow
1. **Ideation** — paste ideation prompt into ChatGPT → generates video ideas across niches
2. **Validate idea** — check YouTube for existing videos on topic to confirm audience interest
3. **Script generation** — paste script prompt into ChatGPT with video idea → full explainer script output
4. **Collect visuals** — read each script line, search Google/YouTube/Pexels for matching images and video clips, download all assets
5. **Voiceover** — use ElevenLabs or Google AI Studio; choose middle-aged male voice with storytelling style; recommends unique voice per channel
6. **Thumbnail generation** — paste thumbnail prompt into ChatGPT with number of items + item names → generates thumbnail image; can request vector style; fit to 16:9 frame
7. **Video editing** (Premiere Pro/CapCut):
   - Create sequence, organize assets in folders per topic
   - Place voiceover on timeline
   - Add white background layer spanning full duration
   - Import thumbnail, add zoom-in animation (Transform effect → keyframe position + scale over 20 frames)
   - Place images/clips matching voiceover line by line
   - Crop video clips, adjust speed if needed
   - Add text overlays (Comic Sans or similar font, black on white)
   - For each new topic: duplicate thumbnail layer, delete old keyframes, animate X-position to center next item
   - Add background music, apply "balanced background music" preset, volume at -15 to -20 dB

### Inputs Required
- Niche/topic selection
- ChatGPT prompts (provided in Google Doc)
- Manual visual collection (images + clips per script line)
- Voice selection
- Editing software

### Video Structure Pattern
- **Intro:** Zoomed-out thumbnail showing all items → zoom into first item
- **Body:** Per item: zoom to item on thumbnail → show relevant images/clips → text overlay with name → voiceover explains
- **Transitions:** Thumbnail pan/zoom between items
- **Audio:** Low background music (-15 to -20 dB) under voiceover
- **Visual style:** White background, images with drop shadows, text below images

### Key Insights
- **"Every X Explained" is a proven viral format** — channels with 16-26 videos getting 100K-800K+ views
- Niches shown: tech, guns/weapons, esports, animals, businesses, Marvel, food/drinks, fashion
- Thumbnail doubles as intro animation (key efficiency trick)
- The manual visual collection step is the most labor-intensive part
- Low barrier to entry but "not easy" — requires patience and determination
- White background aesthetic is signature of this format
- Comic Sans is commonly used font in this niche

### Three Distinct Tiers of Explainer Video Creation

1. **One-Click (NotebookLM):** Feed sources → get video. Zero customization but instant. Best for learning, internal use, content repurposing.
2. **Template-Based (CreateStudio):** Prompt → AI generates timeline → manual brand refinement. Good middle ground.
3. **Manual Assembly (ChatGPT + Editor):** Most control, most labor. Script + manual visual sourcing + frame-by-frame editing. Highest quality ceiling.

### Video Structure Frameworks for Explainers

**Framework 1: Hook-Problem-Solution-CTA (CreateStudio/V1)**
1. Hook — grab attention
2. Problem — identify pain point
3. Solution — present your offering
4. CTA — direct next step

**Framework 2: "Every X Explained" Format (V3, V4)**
1. Thumbnail overview (all items visible)
2. Zoom into item 1 → explain with images/clips
3. Pan to item 2 → explain
4. Repeat for all items
5. Outro

**Framework 3: Narrated Slideshow (NotebookLM/V2, V5)**
1. AI-determined intro with hook
2. Dynamic slides per subtopic (diagrams, quotes, numbers, images)
3. Natural conversational transitions
4. Comprehensive coverage (~1 min per source)

### What Craftit Can Learn From These Workflows

1. **The "Every X Explained" format is massively viral and underserved by AI tools.** No single tool handles the full workflow — creators are stitching together 4-5 tools manually. Craftit could own this entire pipeline.

2. **The hardest bottleneck is visual sourcing.** Every manual-approach creator emphasized that finding and placing images per script line is the most tedious step. Automating visual matching to script lines is the highest-value feature Craftit could offer.

3. **NotebookLM sets the floor for "good enough."** One-click generation from sources is the baseline expectation. Craftit must match this ease while offering more customization (branding, visual style, layout control).

4. **Brand customization is the gap.** NotebookLM has zero branding options. CreateStudio allows it but requires manual work. A tool that auto-applies brand colors, fonts, and logos to generated explainers would be differentiated.

5. **The thumbnail-as-intro pattern is a design insight.** The "Every X Explained" format uses the thumbnail as the video's visual anchor, zooming into each item. This is a reusable template Craftit could offer.

6. **Script structure templates matter.** Hook→Problem→Solution→CTA is the proven formula. Craftit should bake this into script generation with clear scene markers.

7. **Voice uniqueness is valued.** Creators want to sound different from each other. Offering voice variety or custom voice cloning is important.

8. **Source quality = output quality.** NotebookLM users discovered this. Craftit should guide users to provide high-quality inputs rather than just accepting any input.

### What Makes a Great Explainer Video vs a Mediocre One

| Great | Mediocre |
|-------|---------|
| Tight hook→problem→solution→CTA structure | Rambling, no clear structure |
| Visuals precisely match what's being said | Generic stock images loosely related |
| Text timing synced perfectly with voiceover | Text appears too early/late |
| Consistent brand colors, fonts, style | Mismatched visual elements |
| Dynamic visuals (diagrams, zooms, transitions) | Static bullet point slides |
| Enthusiastic, clear narration with storytelling tone | Monotone, robotic delivery |
| Background music low (-15 to -20 dB), non-distracting | Music too loud or absent |
| White/clean background (for "explained" format) | Cluttered backgrounds |
| Each topic gets dedicated visual treatment | Same layout repeated without variation |
| Sources are high-quality and topically focused | Thin or unfocused source material |

## Summary

The explainer video space in 2026 spans from one-click AI generation (NotebookLM) to manual multi-tool assembly (ChatGPT + voice AI + editor). The massive opportunity for Craftit is in the middle: **automated generation with brand customization and visual-to-script matching** — the exact gap no current tool fills well. The "Every X Explained" format is proven viral with channels hitting hundreds of thousands of views on <30 videos, yet requires the most manual effort to produce. Solving that workflow end-to-end would be Craftit's strongest play in the EDUCATE category.
